Part A – Case Study (Phishing Attack)
Title: Phishing Scam Targeting College Students
Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, or bank details by pretending to be a trusted entity.

In a common scenario, a college student receives an email or SMS that appears to be from their university or a popular platform like a bank or internship portal. The message usually contains an urgent request such as “Verify your account” or “Update your details to avoid suspension,” along with a link.

Step-by-step, the attack works as follows:

The attacker sends a fake message/email.
The victim clicks on the provided link.
The link redirects to a fake website that looks real.
The victim enters login credentials or financial details.
The attacker captures this data and misuses it.
Students are often targeted because they frequently use emails, online payments, and are less aware of cybersecurity practices.

The consequences can be severe. Victims may lose money, have their social media or bank accounts hacked, or face identity theft. In some cases, attackers may further exploit compromised accounts to scam others.

This highlights the importance of verifying links, avoiding suspicious messages, and staying aware of online threats.
